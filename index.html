Why Programming?

One of the best descriptions I’ve heard of the emotions around programming is that one is usually in one of the following two states:

  1. Frustration as one grapple some problem in one’s code.
  2. Triumph when one solves the problem.

And that's a large part of why I want to pursue a career in programming: because it’s challenging, interesting, and rewarding; because it stretches both my logical and creative muscles; and because I’d rather feel frustration and triumph by turns than feel neither at all.
